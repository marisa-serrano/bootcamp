Google.org Impact Challenges

Each challenge is an invitation for ideas that tackle a specific problem or support a particular community, whether that is a city, continent, or the entire world. We look for nonprofits and social enterprises with ideas that can create change at the pace and scale the world needs today. The best and boldest ideas are given a strategic package of funding, mentorship, and technical support.

How it Works

  1. Phase 1

    Applications open

    We ask communities for their ideas. Applicants have a specified time to submit their proposals.

  2. Phase 2

    Ideas are selected

    We ask experts, community leaders, and the general public to help determine which ideas have the most potential for impact.

  3. Phase 3

    Announcement and celebration

    Chosen applicants are announced and the community is celebrated.

  4. Phase 4

    Ongoing support

    Awarded nonprofits and social enterprises receive grant funding and support from Google.org for up to three years to turn their ideas into reality.

Two times the impact

For every $1 of investment made through a Google.org Impact Challenge, grantees yielded an additional $1 in funding, which they directly attribute to their participation in the challenge.

Why we created Google.org Impact Challenges

  • We know the people closest to the problem often have the best solutions

    We empower those most closely connected to communities in need to define the problem–and the solution.

  • Participatory philanthropy uncovers new approaches

    We ask experts, social innovators, and the general public to think about difficult problems together, which enables the discovery of new solutions.

  • New ideas need early support to reach their potential

    We provide funding, mentorship, and technical expertise to catalyze ideas that are already making a difference in communities and have the potential to change the world.
